Electronics & Weight Distribution

Electronics
  • Securely mounted onto a sheet of polycarbonate behind the robot with easy access to the kill switch and Crcduino.
    • Battery
  • Placed on end of robot base/frame to counterweight components such as the lead screw lift system which are located at the front.

    • Drive Systems

    Current Drive System
  • Shown in the 3D design: our first iteration.
  • Powered by high speed and torque BaneBot motor (allows robot to easily maneuver the field.)
    • BaneBot Motors
  • Mounted rigidly onto base with sprockets
  • Each sprocket comes with a chain drive directly powering each wheel, giving us a 4 wheel drive.
    • Final Drive System
  • Changed to a gobilda yellow jacket bevel gear mechanism
  • Easier replacement of parts
  • More reliable
  • Compatibile with arduino code

    • Screw Lift

    Lead Screw Mechanisms
  • Powered by a gobilda yellow jacket motor: allows claw and other components of pick-up system to translate upward and downward
  • Easy access to game pieces and loading to the launching system.

    • Launching System

  • Made up of 2 main parts:
  • Guiding Parabole (walls)
  • High RPM flywheel (powered by a gobilda yellow jacket motor)
  • Funnels game pieces from claw after pick-up and, one at a time, shoots them to the desired distance
  • Shoots at a 45 degree angle thanks to the flywheel to achieve a landing on the center post.

    • The Claw

  • Made up 3 different main parts: the stable finger, the moving finger and the flip system.
  • Claw itself is a rack and pinion mechanism
  • 3D printed claw: designed in a way that it can easily access all dispensers on the playing field & drop one gp at a time into the launching system, even if it is holding multiple.
    • Stable Finger
  • Made up of a circular 3D printed part: holds up to 3 game pieces at a time
    • Moving Finger
  • Extends outward and pushes gp’s into the stable finger (all powered by a high torque servo motor.)
    • Flip System
  • Midpoint between claw and lift mechanism
  • Mounted onto lead screw and attached to the claw: allows it to rotate the claw a full 360 degrees either way
  • Dispenses gp’s into the launching mechanism smoothly.

    • Do not hesitate to refer to the CRC website for further explanations on the rulebook.
