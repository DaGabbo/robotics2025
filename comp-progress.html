Brainstorm & First Plans

Building The Base & Adding Rive Motors

BaneBot Motors Issue

  • 1st issue: mounting and placement of our BaneBot motors for the drive system: reduced robot dimensions compared to previous years. Each motor would take up to much space for a direct drive system.
  • 1st solution: extend the base to the maximum robot dimensions possible: not enough to solve the problem.
  • 2nd solution: misplace motor along the frame and use chain drive to keep the same power and speed as a direct drive system.

    • Claw Mechanism Issue

    We started of with the idea of using a very similar system to the vex pro claw but with custom 3d printed parts and attachments to make it work for us.

  • Even through multiple prototypes, we were not able to find a practical way to incorporate all the functions we wanted.
  • Switched to a more basic but practical system: the rack and pinion
  • Eventually we were able to find a good 3D designed claw that would accomplish all our needs

    • Testing & Driving

    BaneBot Issue #2

    BaneBot drive system at first presented many issues;

  • Response time delay,
  • Response to certain code
  • Voltage drops
  • Our steps for solving this issue
  • 1. Redo all wiring connected to the motors (including speed controllers) to rule out any wiring problems.
  • 2. Updated and tweaked the code many times
  • 3. Connecting all motor pins to one node on our terminal block reducing lag and delay
  • 4. Switch to the gobilda yellow jacket bevel gear mechanism due to reliability reasons and lack of solutions to our BaneBot drive system.
  • In the end the gobilda system turned out a lot better than the BaneBot system we had came up with.

    • Launch System

    Launch Mechanism Issue

    First design came with a couple of issues:

  • 1. Underperformance of the flywheel; was making contact with the plexi-glass under.
  • Changed the mounting method: more sturdy and less vibrations across the robot.
  • 2. Installing a perfect semi circle wall for the launcher came with it’s difficulties.
  • We installed a working plexiglass wall after 2 different tries.
  • 3. The suction effect that the high RPM fly would cause against the flywheel
  • Resolved by cutting out a hole the size of the flywheel right under its original mounting position.
